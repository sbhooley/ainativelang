# Localization notes (non-English audiences)

This course is **English-first** but structured so translators and facilitators can adapt without rewriting the whole idea.

**Keep AINL anchors consistent:** fictional names may localize, but the **technical beats** (memory = **graph memory**, saved playbook = **GraphPatch**, approval = **human gate** / **branch**, timeline = **audit trail**, API chaos = **adapter failure** + **branch**) should stay tied to the same ideas in your language.

---

### What travels well

- **Metaphors:** map vs paragraph, recipe/chef, airport translator, subway map — all culture-portable with local examples.  
- **Jordan / Sunrise Ceramics:** fictional; rename shop and characters for regional relatability **without** changing the technical beats (memory, patch, safety, audit, failure).  
- **Short episodes:** translate **episode-by-episode** for podcasts or WhatsApp drip campaigns.

---

### What needs care

- **Idioms:** “page six of a prompt,” “Black Friday,” “pile on social media” — replace with local equivalents (busy season, year-end sale).  
- **Roles:** RACI / finance / legal mentions assume generic corporate defaults—adjust titles (`Sam` → local naming; job titles match your org).  
- **Regulatory framing:** audit and banking references are **illustrative**; swap for your jurisdiction’s vocabulary when teaching compliance audiences.

---

### Suggested workflow for translators

1. Translate **START-HERE-8-MINUTES.md** + **SUNRISE-CERAMICS-STORY.md** first (highest ROI).  
2. Then **CHEAT-SHEET** + **GLOSSARY** (consistent term choices).  
3. **COMPLETE-COURSE.md** last—longest; split by module files if helpful.

---

### Keeping terms consistent

Pick one target language convention early:

- **AINL** — often kept as **AINL** (proper noun).  
- **GraphPatch** — explain once as “saved winning procedure” then use your translated short phrase consistently.

---

### RTL languages

Markdown renders RTL in many modern viewers when document language is set; test PDF/HTML export with a native speaker—**tables** in ROLES doc may need layout tweaks.
