# AINativeLang 101 — Non-Technical Mini Course

**Subtitle:** *How AI workflows become reliable, reusable, and cheaper to run — without reading compiler code.*

This folder holds a **plain-English** introduction to **AINL** (AI Native Language): the workflow language, compiler, runtime, adapters, memory, and how it fits into real products.

**Stories are illustrations, not the syllabus.** The **Sunrise Ceramics** scenes always pair with **named AINL mechanics** (graph, **compile/validate**, **runtime**, **adapters**, **memory**, **GraphPatch**, **human gates**, **audit trail**, **branches**). You should never have to guess what part of AINL a paragraph refers to—see the episode table at the top of `SUNRISE-CERAMICS-STORY.md`.

## Who this is for

- Business owners, product people, operators, investors  
- Sales/marketing and anyone who needs to **explain AINL** without coding  
- New teammates onboarding to an AI/agent stack  

## What was added beyond the original outline

The expanded course keeps the same friendly tone and adds material teams often ask about:

- **Safety rails** — approvals, permissions, “stop and ask a human”  
- **Audit trails** — why structured workflows are easier to explain than prompt soup  
- **When things break** — graceful failure, escalation, and retries (conceptually)  
- **Privacy & trust** — data boundaries in everyday language  
- **Team playbook** — who owns a workflow, versioning, and handoffs  
- **Myths vs facts** — quick reframes for skeptical stakeholders  
- **Where AINL runs** — high-level fit with agent OS / tooling ecosystems  
- **Responsible use** — short, non-preachy ethics checklist
