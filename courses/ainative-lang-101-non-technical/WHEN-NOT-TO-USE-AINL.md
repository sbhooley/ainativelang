# When AINL is the wrong hammer

This page is about **when not to model work as an AINL-style graph** (structured workflow, compile/validate, runtime). Fiction elsewhere in this folder is optional — **these criteria apply to real AINL use.**

AINL shines when work is **repeatable**, **governed**, and **tool-connected**. It is **not** mandatory for every flicker of AI use. Saying so out loud builds trust.

---

### Reach for chat / a simple prompt instead when:

- **One-off brainstorm** — naming ideas, mood-board copy, a single email draft with no policy blast radius.  
- **Pure exploration** — “what are ten angles on this headline?” with no obligation to run again tomorrow the same way.  
- **Human-only judgment** — hiring decisions, clinical calls, legal strategy where **no** automation policy should touch it yet.  
- **Tiny tasks** — five sentences where drawing a map takes longer than doing the task once.

---

### Pause before structuring when:

- **You don’t know your own process.** First messy human runs; **then** capture the winning path.  
- **Rules change weekly** and nobody owns updates — structure without ownership becomes abandoned signage.  
- **Your org won’t assign approvals** — maps with fake “approve” steps that everyone ignores are theater.

---

### Honest limitations (conceptual)

- AINL doesn’t replace **good policies** or **kind humans**.  
- It doesn’t guarantee **correct** answers from models—**visibility and control** improve; magic doesn’t.  
- **Garbage in** (bad steps, bad data access) still produces **garbage out**—just with better receipts.

---

### When AINL tends to **earn its keep**

- The same kind of Tuesday keeps arriving (**refunds, triage, reporting, onboarding**).  
- **Money, mail, or customer data** moves—and you need **stops** and **proof**.  
- More than one person must run the process **the same way**.  
- You’re tired of paying to **re-teach** the model in tokens every run.

---

### One-liner for skeptics

**“We are not structuring your whole life—only the operations that deserve a playbook.”**
