# One-Page Cheat Sheet — AINL for Meetings

The **Sunrise Ceramics** stories in this folder are **deliberately labeled**: each episode names the **AINL mechanism** it stands for (memory, GraphPatch, gates, audit path, failure branches). Use this sheet when you want **terms without fiction**.

## The one-sentence pitch

**AINL turns AI work into structured, reusable workflow maps—so execution is more predictable, inspectable, and often cheaper than mega-prompting.**

## The problem in 10 seconds

Long prompts are expensive, inconsistent, and hard to govern. Teams need **repeatable operations**, not one-off creativity for everything.

## The three-layer story

1. **Write/govern** — Express the workflow as a structured plan (not a paragraph).  
2. **Compile/check** — Convert it into an official internal map you can validate.  
3. **Run** — Execute the map with tools (adapters), memory, branches, and audit-friendly behavior.

## What to say instead of jargon

| Jargon | Friendly phrase |
|--------|-------------------|
| Graph IR | “Official workflow map” |
| Runtime | “Engine that runs the map” |
| Adapters | “Certified tool connections” |
| Validation | “Preflight checks” |
| GraphPatch | “Save the winning procedure to the playbook” |

## Differentiators (non-technical)

- **Vs. prompts alone:** Structure + reuse + governance.  
- **Vs. rigid automation:** Keeps room for AI judgment where it belongs.  
- **Vs. mystery agents:** Easier to explain “what happened” when steps are explicit.

## Questions stakeholders actually ask

**“Will it replace our team?”**  
No—it turns tribal knowledge into repeatable procedures your team supervises.

**“Is it safe?”**  
Safety is a product/design choice: approvals, permissions, boundaries—and AINL-style workflows make those controls easier to enforce than prompt soup.

**“Why save money?”**  
You stop paying to re-teach the same workflow in tokens every run; the structure lives in the graph.

## Close with a picture

Prompt-only AI is a **speech**.  
AINL is a **map** with **tool hooks**, **memory**, and **saved wins**.
