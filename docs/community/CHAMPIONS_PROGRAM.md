# AINL Champions Program

The AINL Champions Program recognizes and rewards community members who make a significant impact on the ecosystem.

---

## 🏆 What is a Champion?

AINL Champions are:

- **Recognized leaders** in the AINL community
- **Active contributors** who help others succeed
- **Ambassadors** who spread the word responsibly
- **Earners of $AINL tokens** for their impact

Champions are not employees. They are respected volunteers who receive benefits in exchange for sustained contributions.

---

## 🌟 Champion Tiers

### 🌱 **Seedling** (Entry Level)

**Requirements:**
- 10+ helpful answers in GitHub Discussions or Discord
- 1+ tutorial or case study published on GitHub or blog
- Active for at least 2 months

**Benefits:**
- 🌱 Champion badge on GitHub profile (comment reactions)
- Early access to beta features (1-week head start)
- Featured in monthly spotlight
- **5,000 $AINL** quarterly reward

---

### 🌿 **Grower** (Intermediate)

**Requirements:**
- 50+ helpful answers over 6 months
- 3+ high-quality tutorials or templates
- Template accepted into official gallery
- Mentor 2+ newcomers (verified by them)

**Benefits:**
- 🌿 Champion badge + profile listing on ainativelang.com
- Direct Slack channel with core team
- Beta feature access + ability to shape roadmap (influence votes)
- **20,000 $AINL** quarterly reward
- Annual swag package (hoodie, stickers, pins)
- Free Business support tier ($300/mo value)

---

### 🌳 **Tree** (Advanced)

**Requirements:**
- Sustained contributions for 12+ months
- 10+ accepted templates or major documentation overhauls
- Presented AINL at meetup/conference (video proof)
- Active mentorship (5+ mentees)

**Benefits:**
- 🌳 Champion badge + full profile on website with bio
- Quarterly video call with core team (strategy input)
- Annual all-expense-paid trip to team summit (location varies)
- **50,000 $AINL** quarterly reward
- Free Enterprise support tier ($1,000/mo value)
- Co-marketing opportunities (guest posts, webinars)
- Token-gated feature access (premium templates, tools)

## 📊 How to Qualify

### Activity Tracking

Champion status is reviewed **quarterly** (Mar, Jun, Sep, Dec). We track:

| Metric | How It's Measured | Weight |
|--------|-------------------|--------|
| Helpful answers | GitHub "solved" reactions, Discord assists | 30% |
| Content quality | Views, forks, citations, peer review | 30% |
| Templates submitted | Accepted into official gallery | 20% |
| Mentorship | Newcomer surveys ("who helped you?") | 10% |
| Community presence | Conference talks, blog posts, streams | 10% |

**Minimum thresholds** must be met in at least 3 of 5 categories for tier upgrade.

### Quality over Quantity

We value **impact**, not just volume. One breakthrough tutorial that helps 100+ people counts more than 100 tiny Discord replies.

Banned or suspended users are ineligible. Toxic behavior disqualifies permanently.

## 🎁 Token Rewards

$AINL tokens are distributed quarterly to champion wallets. Token economics:

- **Seedling**: 5,000 $AINL / quarter
- **Grower**: 20,000 $AINL / quarter
- **Tree**: 50,000 $AINL / quarter

**Additional bonuses:**
- Template featured on homepage: +10,000 $AINL
- Tutorial receives >1,000 views: +2,500 $AINL
- Conference talk: +25,000 $AINL (once per event)
- Mentorship sprint (10+ mentees in month): +5,000 $AINL

Tokens are vesting over 1 year to encourage sustained participation (25% immediate, 25% each quarter).

## 🎯 Champion Responsibilities

Champions are expected to:

1. **Be helpful** – Answer questions patiently, especially from beginners
2. **Be respectful** – No harassment, gatekeeping, or toxic behavior
3. **Shine responsibly** – Represent AINL accurately; no hype or speculation
4. **Mentor** – Help newcomers get their first agent working
5. **Provide feedback** – Report bugs, usability issues, and improvement ideas
6. **Stay current** – Keep up with AINL releases and changes
7. **Disclose** – If you're paid to endorse AINL, say so

### What Champions Are NOT

- ❌ Official customer support (you still use support@ for production issues)
- ❌ Legal advisors ( don't give compliance advice unless you're qualified)
- ❌ Company representatives (unless you work for AINL, then you're staff, not champion)
- ❌ Token promoters (no price speculation, no "buy now" messaging)

## 🔄 Review Process

1. **Application window**: Open year-round
2. **Review cycle**: Quarterly (ends last day of Mar/Jun/Sep/Dec)
3. **Evaluation**: Core team + existing champions review contributions
4. **Decision**: Within 30 days of cycle end
5. **Announcement**: Blog post + Discord announcement + Twitter/X

### Rejection? Keep contributing!

Most champions are approved on 2nd or 3rd application. Use feedback to improve.

---

## ⚠️ Code of Conduct & Removal

Champions must adhere to the [AINL Code of Conduct](https://github.com/sbhooley/ainativelang/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).

**Grounds for removal:**
- Toxicity, harassment, or discrimination
- Misrepresenting themselves as official AINL employees
- Token dumping or encouraging speculation
- Sustained inactivity (>6 months without contribution)
- Sharing confidential information (beta leaks, customer data)

Removal is at the discretion of the core team. Appeals go to the full champion panel (unanimous vote required to reinstate).

## ❓ FAQ

**Q: Can I be a champion if I work for a competitor?**  
A: Yes, as long as you adhere to the code of conduct and don't share proprietary info. Disclose your affiliation.

**Q: What if I go on hiatus?**  
A: Up to 6 months inactivity is fine. Beyond that, you may be moved to "inactive" status (no token rewards until active again).

**Q: Can I sell my tokens immediately?**  
A: Yes, but we encourage staking to participate in governance and access token-gated features.

**Q: What prevents champions from just spamming?**  
A: Quality metrics. Low-value contributions don't count toward thresholds. We review manually.

**Q: How do I prove I helped someone?**  
A: Use GitHub Reactions (👀, ❤️, 🎉), Discord `/thanks` command, or have them fill out a survey.

**Q: Do I need to be technical?**  
A: Not necessarily. Great teachers, documenters, and community managers are valuable too.
