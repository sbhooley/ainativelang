# Plan Your Week

A quick Sunday evening session to review the week ahead, set your top priorities, and walk into Monday feeling prepared.

## Steps

1. **Review upcoming calendar** — Use your **calendar skill** to pull all events, meetings, and appointments for the next 7 days.

2. **Identify busy days** — Flag days with back-to-back meetings or heavy schedules. Note any days with open blocks for deep work.

3. **Check for conflicts** — Look for double-bookings, overlapping events, or meetings that might need to be moved.

4. **Set top 3 priorities** — Ask the user: "What are the 3 most important things you want to accomplish this week?" If they have existing goals or projects, reference those.

5. **Prep for Monday** — Highlight what's happening tomorrow specifically. Early meetings, deadlines, or things to prepare the night before.

6. **Share the plan** — Deliver a clean summary of the week ahead.

## Output Format

```
Week Ahead — [date range]

Monday: 3 meetings, project deadline
Tuesday: Light day — good for deep work
Wednesday: Team standup, dentist at 2pm
Thursday: All-day workshop
Friday: 1-on-1 with manager, wrap-up

Top 3 Priorities:
  1. Finish project proposal
  2. Schedule annual checkup
  3. Clean out garage

Monday Prep:
  - 9am standup (have status update ready)
  - Project proposal draft due by EOD
  - Pack lunch — busy morning
```

## Notes

- This works best when the user has connected their calendar. If not, ask them to share what's on their plate.
- Keep the tone encouraging — Sunday evenings should feel like getting organized, not getting stressed.
- If the user seems overloaded, gently suggest what could be moved or delegated.
- Three priorities max. More than that and nothing is really a priority.
